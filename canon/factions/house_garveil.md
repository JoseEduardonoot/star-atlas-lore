**House Garveil** is the scholar house of the [Sogmian](../species/sogmian.md) Noble Houses — the azure flame that illuminates where [House Xictus](../factions/house_xictus.md) leads the charge and [House Busan](../factions/house_busan.md) builds. Like all Sogmian houses, Garveil is a warrior house — every member trains with weapons from childhood and carries at least one blade at all times. A Garveil scientist can kill as readily as any Xictus front-liner. But their true weapon is the mind, and their motto — *"Repetition is the mother of learning"* — reveals their character: methodical, patient, devoted to mastery through practice.

The house produces researchers, experimentalists, and systematic thinkers who have advanced Sogmian technology from first wheels to Titan-class warships.

---

## Scholar-Warriors

Every Sogmian is a warrior. The [Du Prah](../species/sogmian.md) and the [Gray Citadel](../factions/sogmian_sovereignty.md) ensure that — all houses share the same martial foundation. Those who join Garveil are no less dangerous than their Xictus peers — they simply channel their discipline toward understanding rather than front-line combat.

This makes Garveil unique among galactic scholarly institutions. Where the [Iris Academy](../factions/iris_academy.md) produces civilian researchers and the [Mierese Lore Keepers](../factions/mierese_lore_keepers.md) preserve knowledge through oral tradition, House Garveil produces scientist-warriors who conduct research with the same intensity that Xictus brings to the front lines.

The house works alongside the [Iris Academy](../factions/iris_academy.md), contributing the scientific expertise that balances Sogmian martial tradition within the broader [ONI Consortium](../factions/oni.md) framework.

## The Photoli Secret — Unlocking the Titan

The single most consequential act in House Garveil's history occurred during the Sogmian exile in the HRZ, following the [Convergence War](../history/eras_of_star_atlas.md).

A **Garveil scholar** — described as a pilgrim seeking knowledge — encountered a **disguised [Photoli](../species/photoli.md)** deep in the HRZ. This Photoli provided the **technology to create the power source** that would make the Titan-class warship possible. House Garveil's scientists translated this alien knowledge into practical engineering specifications that [House Busan](../factions/house_busan.md) then used to construct *The Last Stand mk. VIII* — the warship that ended the [Convergence War](../history/eras_of_star_atlas.md) by its mere appearance.

This event raises profound questions:

- **Why did the Photoli intervene?** The [Photoli](../species/photoli.md) are described as *"witnesses and wardens, lending presence if not hands"* — yet this act was a direct, material intervention that reshaped galactic history.
- **Who was the disguised Photoli?** Their identity has never been confirmed. The [Photoli species](../factions/the_photoli.md) has not officially acknowledged this event.
- **What was Garveil's role in the translation?** Converting alien energy principles into workable engineering required not just intelligence but a particular kind of methodical patience — precisely the quality Garveil was founded to cultivate.

The debt is Sogmian; the motive is Photoli. And House Garveil sits at the intersection of both.

## Role Within ONI

House Garveil serves as the scientific counterweight to ONI's martial tendencies. Within the Consortium's structure:

- **Research & Development**: Garveil scholars advance ship technology, weapons systems, and medical sciences for all ONI species
- **Intelligence Analysis**: The methodical Garveil mind excels at pattern recognition and strategic assessment
- **Academic Exchange**: Garveil maintains collaborative relationships with non-Sogmian institutions, particularly the [Iris Academy](../factions/iris_academy.md)
- **Titan Maintenance**: The knowledge required to operate and maintain the Titan's power source remains primarily within Garveil expertise

### The Scholar

Karveth entered the [Gray Citadel](../factions/sogmian_sovereignty.md) like every Sogmian — and by every measurable standard, he was unremarkable. His combat scores were average. His physical endurance was middling. His weapons instructor — Darneth of [House Xictus](../factions/house_xictus.md), who would later become Iron-Forge Instructor — gave Karveth a single assessment: *"Adequate."*

What Darneth did not assess was what happened after the training hall closed. Karveth stayed. Every night. Not to drill — to disassemble. He took apart his training weapon, his armor joints, the locking mechanism on the armory door, and sometimes the scoring machinery itself. He put everything back together before dawn. When asked why, his answer became one of the most repeated lines in Garveil recruit lore: *"I cannot fight what I do not understand. And I do not yet understand anything."*

By the time the [Convergence War](../history/convergence_war.md) erupted (~2512), Karveth was a methodologist — a Garveil researcher specializing in the systematic analysis of alien technologies. He was fourteen years old when his house dispatched him to catalog captured enemy equipment. He was twenty-two when he translated his first non-Sogmian engineering schematic. He was thirty when he was assigned to the most sensitive project in Sogmian history.

### The Keeper

Karveth is one of only three living Sogmians who fully understands the Titan's power source.

The original translation — the conversion of [Photoli](../species/photoli.md) energy principles into workable engineering — was performed by the legendary **Maerov 𐎌 Garveil**, the pilgrim-scholar who encountered the disguised Photoli in the HRZ. Maerov's notes were brilliant but incomplete — the kind of intuitive leaps that a genius makes in the moment and fails to document afterward. When [House Busan](../factions/house_busan.md) needed precise specifications to actually construct *The Last Stand*, it was a young Karveth who sat with Maerov's scrawled equations for three years and turned fragmentary insight into systematic engineering.

This makes Karveth irreplaceable — and he knows it. The Titan's power source cannot be maintained, repaired, or replicated without Garveil expertise, and within Garveil, no one understands the system as deeply as Karveth. He has spent decades training successors, writing documentation, creating redundancies — because the last thing a methodologist wants is to be the single point of failure.

But the knowledge carries a weight that documentation cannot transfer. Karveth knows *what* the Photoli gave. He also knows — or strongly suspects — *why*. And he has never shared this suspicion with anyone outside House Garveil's inner circle.

### The Burden

The Photoli did not give the Sogmians a weapon. They gave them a **question**.

The energy principles behind the Titan's power source are not [Photoli](../species/photoli.md) technology — they are Photoli *philosophy* expressed as mathematics. The equations describe energy as something that is observed into existence — a system where the act of measurement is the act of creation. This is consistent with everything known about the Photoli as a species: beings who believe that witnessing is the highest form of existence.

Karveth has spent eighty years with these equations. He has come to believe that the Photoli intervention was not an act of charity. It was a **test**. The Photoli wanted to see what the Sogmians — a species defined by action, by the Du Prah's imperative to intervene — would do when given a tool built on the philosophy of observation. Would they use it to fight? To dominate? Or would they eventually understand that the power source works because *someone is watching* — and ask the terrifying follow-up question: *Who?*

Karveth believes that the Titan's power source is sustained by something that is aware of it. He cannot prove this. He has no empirical evidence beyond mathematical inference. But the suspicion has shaped his entire leadership: cautious, methodical, deeply reluctant to allow anyone to modify or experiment with the Titan's core systems.

*"We did not build this weapon. We were given it. And I am not certain we understand the terms."*

### The Leader

Karveth was elected Kur'faist in ~2575 — five years before Gorvath assumed leadership of [House Xictus](../factions/house_xictus.md). The two have worked in parallel for over forty years, and their relationship is the intellectual backbone of modern Sogmian governance.

Where Gorvath is clipped and direct, Karveth is deliberate and layered. Where Gorvath makes a decision and commits, Karveth makes a decision, reconsiders it three times, tests it against available data, and then commits — with contingencies for every failure mode he can imagine. This exasperates Xictus officers and delights Garveil subordinates.

As Kur'faist, Karveth has:

- **Expanded the Iris Academy partnership**, embedding Garveil researchers in the Academy's programs and inviting non-Sogmian scholars into Garveil's laboratories for the first time
- **Documented the Titan's power source** systematically — creating the first complete technical manual, copies of which are held by Garveil, [House Busan](../factions/house_busan.md), and the [ONI Consortium](../factions/oni.md) leadership
- **Opposed the Sov Arei vacancy** — not on political grounds, but on methodological ones. *"A system without a leader is an experiment without a control. You cannot measure the health of the Sogmian civilization while a critical variable remains undefined."*
- **Maintained absolute secrecy** about his suspicion regarding the Titan's power source. Even Gorvath does not know the full depth of Karveth's concern.

### Personality

Karveth speaks slowly. Not from hesitation — from precision. Every word is chosen. Every sentence is structured to convey exactly the intended meaning and no more. Conversations with Karveth feel like reading a research paper narrated aloud.

He is **patient beyond reason** — a quality that makes him nearly impossible to provoke and deeply frustrating to argue with. He will listen to an opponent's position, acknowledge its merits, identify its flaws, and present his counter-argument with such methodical clarity that the opponent often agrees before realizing they've been persuaded. [House Lutavira](../factions/house_lutavira.md)'s political operators consider him the most dangerous negotiator in the Sogmian Sovereignty — not because he is cunning, but because he is *right*, and he can prove it.

His relationship with Gorvath is one of the most important bonds in modern Sogmian governance. They disagree frequently — on pace, on risk tolerance, on the value of action versus analysis — but they trust each other absolutely. Gorvath once described the partnership: *"I tell Karveth what needs to be done. Karveth tells me why I'm wrong. Then we find the third answer together."*

On [House Akalma](../factions/house_akalma.md)'s return: Karveth has taken no public position. Privately, he has begun researching the Vega Fall archives — applying the same methodical scrutiny to a century-old political event that he applies to energy equations. He suspects the official account is incomplete. He has not yet determined what it is missing.

## The Garveil Officers

Each role within House Garveil produces a different kind of scholar-warrior — and a different relationship with knowledge, the Du Prah, and the Sogmian identity.

### Thalira 𐏕 Garveil Mar'graf Kaa — Shield-Scholar

> *"The enemy does not care about your equations. But your equations will tell you where the enemy will be standing in thirty seconds."*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Rank** | Mar'graf Kaa (Shield-Scholar — Garveil's term for Shield-Captain) |
| **Born** | ~2555 (Segal Sector) |
| **Age** | ~65 (current era ~2620) |
| **Lance** | *Blue Theorem* — 10 warriors, assigned to high-value asset protection |

**Thalira** commands the lance responsible for protecting Garveil's most sensitive research facilities and personnel. Unlike Xictus shield-captains who lead charges, Thalira fights *defensively* — her tactical philosophy is entirely about prediction, positioning, and denying the enemy any engagement on their terms.

**Worldview — War Is an Equation**: Thalira believes combat is a solved problem. Not that fighting is easy — but that every engagement can be reduced to variables: terrain, force ratios, morale coefficients, supply lines, reaction times. Her fighters train with data overlays. Her pre-battle briefings read like academic papers. She has never lost an engagement — but she has also never fought against a force that could not be modeled.

This makes her brilliant in conventional operations and potentially catastrophic against anything genuinely unpredictable. Karveth knows this and keeps Thalira on defensive assignments where predictability is an asset. The question is what happens when the unpredictable arrives.

### Orvenn 𐏕 Garveil Rat'Prah — Vow-Keeper

> *"The Du Prah says we may use violence only to cease violence. But what about the violence of ignorance? What about the violence of a truth left unspoken?"*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Rank** | Rat'Prah (Vow-Keeper) |
| **Born** | ~2510 (Segal Sector) |
| **Age** | ~110 (current era ~2620) |
| **Assignment** | Kur'faist's inner council — ethical oversight of all research programs |

**Orvenn** is the only Sogmian Vow-Keeper who interprets the Du Prah through an epistemological lens. Where Xictus Vow-Keepers vet military operations, Orvenn vets *knowledge* — asking whether a particular research program, discovery, or technological advance complies with the Sogmian moral code.

**Worldview — Knowledge Can Be Violence**: Orvenn believes that withholding critical knowledge from those who need it is itself a form of violence — an act that causes harm through inaction. This puts him in direct tension with Karveth's secrecy about the Titan's power source. Orvenn does not know the full depth of Karveth's suspicions, but he senses something is being withheld, and his entire philosophy tells him that withholding is morally equivalent to lying.

*"If we know the Titan is sustained by something aware, and we do not tell the other houses, then every day we maintain that silence is a day we commit violence against the truth."*

He and Karveth have argued this point for decades — always in private, always with respect, and always without resolution. It is the defining philosophical tension within House Garveil's leadership.

### Nalith 𐏕 Garveil Frei'har Sen — Gate-Watch

> *"I guard this laboratory the way Velka guards her stargate. The difference is that what I protect is already inside."*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Rank** | Frei'har Sen (Gate-Watch) |
| **Born** | ~2560 (Segal Sector) |
| **Age** | ~60 (current era ~2620) |
| **Post** | Garveil Primary Research Complex — Segal Sector |

**Nalith** guards the physical entrance to Garveil's most classified research facilities — the laboratories where the Titan's power source documentation is stored and where the most sensitive experiments are conducted.

**Worldview — Understanding Is Overrated**: Nalith is a Garveil warrior who does not care about science. She finds this hilarious, and no one in House Garveil finds it funny at all. She chose Garveil not for its intellectual tradition but because *"the azure looked better on me than obsidian."* She trained as a warrior, excels as a sentinel, and has absolutely no interest in what happens behind the doors she guards.

This makes her — paradoxically — the most trusted guard in the house. She cannot be bribed with knowledge because she does not want it. She cannot be manipulated by intellectual arguments because she does not engage with them. She cannot be compromised by curiosity because she has none. She is a lock that has no interest in the key.

Karveth values Nalith precisely for this quality. *"She is proof that the Sogmian warrior tradition produces something that transcends specialization. She does not need to understand what she protects. She only needs to protect it."*

### Dessik 𐏕 Garveil Her'graf Tahl — Forge-Mind Instructor

> *"Darneth teaches them to question the Du Prah. I teach them to question reality. His job is harder. Mine is more dangerous."*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Rank** | Her'graf Tahl (Forge-Mind Instructor — Garveil's variant) |
| **Born** | ~2530 (HRZ — born during Exile) |
| **Age** | ~90 (current era ~2620) |
| **Assignment** | [Gray Citadel](../factions/sogmian_sovereignty.md) — scientific method instructor |

**Dessik** works alongside Darneth of [House Xictus](../factions/house_xictus.md) in the [Gray Citadel](../factions/sogmian_sovereignty.md) — but where Darneth teaches combat, Dessik teaches *thinking*. Every Sogmian cadet learns the scientific method from a Garveil instructor. Dessik's course is mandatory, and it is legendary for being the most intellectually demanding experience in the Citadel — harder than weapons training, harder than endurance trials.

**Worldview — Curiosity Is the Only Du Prah That Matters**: Dessik believes that the Sogmians' greatest strength is not their martial discipline or their moral code — it is their refusal to accept mystery. A Sogmian who encounters something they do not understand does not worship it, ignore it, or run from it. They *study* it. This, Dessik argues, is the real Du Prah — the Vow of Worth expressed as the commitment to understand the universe on its own terms.

This view is borderline heretical to [House Lutavira](../factions/house_lutavira.md)'s Du Prah fundamentalists, who believe the Vows are about moral conduct, not epistemology. Dessik does not care. He has taught four generations of Sogmians to think before they act, measure before they judge, and — most importantly — to say *"I don't know"* before they claim certainty.

His friendship with Darneth is the most interesting inter-house relationship in the Gray Citadel. They teach in adjacent halls and share meals every evening. Darneth teaches doubt about morality; Dessik teaches doubt about reality. Together, they produce Sogmian graduates who are slow to swing, slow to judge — and absolutely lethal when they finally commit.

### Yesska 𐏕 Garveil Kur'faist Vek — The Sapphire Ring

> *"We are not the Obsidian Hand. We don't guard the Kur'faist's body — we guard his mind. If someone gets past us, they won't kill him. They'll convince him of something untrue. And that is worse."*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Rank** | Kur'faist Vek (Sapphire Ring — Garveil's variant of the personal guard) |
| **Born** | ~2500 (Segal Sector) |
| **Age** | ~120 (current era ~2620) |
| **Assignment** | Kur'faist Karveth's personal guard — five scholar-warriors |

**Yesska** commands the **Sapphire Ring** — Garveil's equivalent of the Obsidian Hand. But where Suroc's Obsidian Hand protects Gorvath from physical threats, Yesska's Sapphire Ring protects Karveth from *intellectual* ones.

The Ring's five members are not merely bodyguards — they are counter-intelligence specialists trained to detect disinformation, identify manipulative arguments, and recognize when someone is attempting to extract classified information through seemingly innocent conversation. In a galaxy where knowledge is Garveil's greatest asset, the theft of that knowledge is the greatest threat.

**Worldview — Trust Is a Security Vulnerability**: Yesska is deeply paranoid — and she is correct to be. The Titan's power source, the Photoli connection, the ongoing research programs — Garveil holds secrets that every faction in the galaxy would pay dearly to obtain. Yesska's operating assumption is that *someone is always trying*, and her job is to ensure they never succeed.

This creates a tension with Karveth's push for academic openness and the [Iris Academy](../factions/iris_academy.md) partnership. Every time Karveth hosts a symposium or admits a foreign scholar into a Garveil laboratory, Yesska assigns a Ring member to shadow them. Karveth tolerates this. Yesska considers it insufficient.

On Gorvath's Obsidian Hand: *"Suroc guards a warrior. Warriors have enemies you can see. I guard a scholar. Scholars have enemies who smile, shake your hand, and steal your life's work while complimenting your research."*

## House Roles

The following roles define the operational structure of House Garveil:

| Role | Title | Function |
|------|-------|----------|
| **House Leader** | Kur'faist | Supreme commander of House Garveil. Leads all house affairs — scientific, military, and diplomatic. Currently held by **Karveth** |
| **Shield-Scholar** | Mar'graf Kaa | Defensive combat officers who protect research assets. Command lances focused on prediction and denial rather than assault |
| **Vow-Keeper** | Rat'Prah | Advisors who vet research programs against the [Du Prah](../species/sogmian.md). Unique to Garveil: epistemological ethics, not just martial ethics |
| **Gate-Watch** | Frei'har Sen | Sentinels who guard classified facilities and research complexes. Trusted specifically for their *lack* of curiosity about what they protect |
| **Forge-Mind Instructor** | Her'graf Tahl | Gray Citadel instructors who teach the scientific method. Every Sogmian passes through their course regardless of house |
| **Sapphire Ring** | Kur'faist Vek | The Kur'faist's personal guard — counter-intelligence specialists who protect against intellectual threats, not just physical ones |
