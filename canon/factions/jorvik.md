## The Pirate King

The Pirate King is a paradox: a leader of anarchists, a commander who despises command, a man who wears a crown to mock the concept of crowns. He is a radical defender of personal freedom who believes the only thing that can bind a sentient being is their own word.

## Organization

### The Nomadic Fleet

The Jorvik have no homeworld, no permanent base, no territory they would die to defend. Their fleet is their nation â€” a constantly moving constellation of ships, stations, and improvised structures. This makes them nearly impossible to destroy: you can defeat a Jorvik fleet, but you cannot occupy Jorvik territory, because no such territory exists.

### Captains and Crews

Every Jorvik captain creates their own moral code â€” as long as it does not violate the Creed. Some captains are honorable raiders who avoid civilian casualties. Others are ruthless opportunists who exploit every angle. The Creed sets the floor, not the ceiling.

### Fairs

In times of great need, the Jorvik hold **Fairs** â€” gatherings where ships connect through improvised bridges and gangways around a random station. Trade, recruitment, dispute resolution, and celebration occur simultaneously.

Every 200 space days, the entire fleet gathers at a randomly selected location for the **Grand Fair** â€” a week-long event that is part festival, part muster, part tribunal. The Pirate King addresses the fleet. Captains resolve disputes. New members are initiated.

### The Brand

New members must prove their worth to a Captain. Upon acceptance, they receive **The Brand** â€” an actual tattoo imprinted at the molecular level, permanent and unforgeable. The Brand identifies any Jorvik to any other Jorvik, anywhere in the galaxy. It cannot be removed, faked, or hidden from the technology used to read it.

This creates a permanent, irrevocable commitment: once branded, always branded.
