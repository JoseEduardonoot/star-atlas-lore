## Overview

**Redam-VII** stands as a testament to the MUD Faction's expansive efforts towards Iris. Although it has since gained independence, Redam has managed to nurture a **harmonious, mutually beneficial** relationship with its MUD originators — a distinction not shared by its sister sectors.

## Key Lore

### Diplomatic Powerhouse

- **Most stable** amongst the MUD ex-colonies.
- **Commerce nexus**: Strategic location bridging trade between the MUD Safe Zone and MRZ systems.
- **Diplomatic role**: Spearheaded negotiations between MUD and Pergamos during the COP offensive crisis.

### Governance

- **Elected leadership**: Five distinguished humans, each from a **distinct planet** within the sector.
- **~2524**: The five leaders' rules are formally installed and **governance becomes stabilized** in the sector.
- **Elected Prime Minister** serves as coordinator.
- Steadfastly guards its **autonomy** against MUD and MRZ entities seeking to extend influence.

### Current Tensions

- Both **MUD leadership** and various MRZ entities have cast covetous eyes on Redam.
- Governance remains vigilant. Stability is ever-elusive outside COP jurisdiction.
- **~2555**: Redam attempts to form a **league of MRZ factions** for mutual cooperation and defense — the effort **fails**, highlighting the deep fragmentation and mistrust between medium zone powers
- Relationship with MUD is **mutually beneficial** but politics are always at play in Galia Expanse.
- **~2623**: Fearing the **COP intervention in the Medium Zone**, Redam reaches out to the **old MUD colonies**, **Zenith Door**, and **Free Harbors** to **pressure the COP for a truce** and limit their military activities — successfully forcing a ceasefire

## Political Influence

- **Alignment**: Pro-MUD (but independent)
- **Role**: Regional diplomat and trade mediator
