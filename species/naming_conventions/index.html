    📛 Canon Document: SPECIES NAMING CONVENTIONS¶

    Status: 🟢 Canon (Established Rules, Feb 2026) Source: Creator-defined naming system Usage: All new character names MUST follow these conventions

    Ustur¶

    Two-part name: Collective Derivation + Enlightenment Step Suffix

    The first name derives from a collective noun (herd, army, flock, colony, etc.). The second name represents advancement through the seven steps of Ustur enlightenment.

    Enlightenment Steps (lowest → highest)¶

    Step Suffix Rarity Tier
    1 .bod Common, Uncommon, Rare
    2 .iddt Uncommon, Rare, Epic
    3 .lrnr Rare, Epic, Legendary
    4 .doer Epic, Legendary
    5 .tchr Legendary, Anomaly
    6 .soul Anomaly
    7 .eldr —

    Examples¶

    Full Name Derivation Step
    Herdus.soul Herd 6
    Armi.eldr Army 7
    Flicer.bod Flock 1
    Calon.tchr Colony 5
    Libra.lrnr Library 3

    Photoli¶

    Photoli recognize each other by unique magnetic fields and don't value names. They adopt short, musical names only to ease communication with other species.

    Rules¶

    • Short — 2-3 letters preferred
    • Musical — vowel-heavy, singable
    • No complex structure

    Examples¶

    Reh, Ahr, Fae, Om, Mer, Doh

    Mierese¶

    Three-part name: Hidden (Private) + Public + Region of Neuno

    • First name (Hidden): Deliberately unpleasant-sounding to non-Mierese. Only close friends and family know it.
    • Second name (Public): Single, catchy, strong word — highly recognizable. Mierese love nicknames.
    • Third name (Region): References the ancient region of Neuno from which their lineage originates.

    MUD (Human)¶

    No fixed naming convention. MUD origins, beliefs, and worldviews are too diverse for a single naming system.

    Punaab¶

    Punaabs (especially High Punaabs) believe names represent status. Parents give children long, complicated names and privately use abbreviations.

    Rules¶

    • Double vowels
    • Excessive letter repetition
    • Grand, multi-syllabic

    Examples¶

    Maartrichanna, Marekenaamatra, Binaree, Pullaraajanethrim, Nopeenfree, Huricanno, Heddaraa, Ayevat, Kikiraajin, Buccheri, Kuropatriprica, Minimimii

    Lumikir¶

    Structure: Compound root-word name (2-3 roots combined)

    The Lumikir are a species built on language — Om gave them writing, and they rebuilt their entire civilization around it. Their names reflect this: each Lumikir name is a compound word assembled from meaningful roots in the Lumikir proto-language. Every syllable carries etymological weight. A Lumikir's name is, in essence, a micro-description of their identity or aspiration.

    Names are typically 2-3 roots combined. Parents choose roots at birth to express their hopes for the child; some Lumikir later rename themselves when they join a guild or achieve a significant contribution, selecting roots that better reflect who they've become.

    Construction Rules¶

    1. Standard name: Action/Quality + Domain + (optional Modifier)
    2. Names are read left-to-right as a phrase: Veloss = "seeker of stars", Toreth = "writer of history"
    3. Some Lumikir use two action/quality roots when no single domain fits: Velkael = "sharp seeker"
    4. Guild affiliation is stated separately, not embedded in the name — but over time, certain root combinations become associated with specific guilds (e.g., Ren- and -unn names gravitate toward the Whisper Conclave)
    5. The modifier -or is reserved for Lumikir who hold council seats — using it without earning it is a serious social offense
