    🦎 Canon Document: MIERESE¶

    Status: 🟢 Canon (Creator-provided, Feb 2026) Source: Direct Creator input — Full Mierese Lore Keeper chapters + Q&A

    Biology & Physiology¶

    • Skin: Lavender/lilac, high puncture resistance (armor rarely needed)
    • Build: Slim, slender frames, pointed ears
    • Motor system: Much richer spectrum of voluntary muscle control than humans (more motor neurons)
    • Strength: Weaker than humans — system evolved for constant movement, not weight-bearing
    • Heat tolerance: Withstand up to 45°C without biological performance loss
    • Poison resistance: Naturally resistant to many toxins (adapted from Neuno's environment)
    • Cybernetic vulnerability: Due to their biological composition, Mierese are susceptible to hacking, EMPs, and other cyber-electronic attacks that target integrated technology. Their biology resists poisons but not electronic warfare
    • Diet: Can digest almost anything except inorganic matter
    • Favorite food: MUD ice cream (to soothe Neuno's heat)

    Head Tentacles (The Tail)¶

    • Multi-functional muscle/organ on the head
    • Functions: body balance, fine physical control, memory repository
    • Divided into many parts with different functions
    • Lore Keepers tend to have even longer ones than average

    Dimorphism¶

    • Male: Extremely long dreadlock-like head tentacles
    • Female: Shorter braid-like tentacles with elaborate cranial crest

    Personality¶

    • Free-spirited, improvisational, cheerful
    • Perceived as chaotic by orderly species (Ustur)
    • Perceived as cowards by honor-bound Sogmians (Mierese view dying for a cause as purposeless)
    • Cope with stress through humor ("Ok, this is how we die")
    • Love bets (like their god, but always aware of every detail)

    Naming Convention (Three Names)¶

    Every Mierese has THREE names:

    Position Type Description
    First Hidden/Private Sounds disgusting/weird to non-Mierese; only close friends and family know it
    Second Public Single, catchy, strong word — highly recognizable in tales
    Third Regional Fixed surname from ancestral Neuno region

    Founding Myth: The Grand Tale¶

    Chapter One — Onato and Juriko¶

    • Onato: Most beautiful and powerful god. Talented (singing, dancing, storytelling, fighting). Prideful but good-hearted. Accepted any bet and never lost
    • Juriko: Envious god who plotted against Onato
    • Juriko organized a competition of challenges among the gods
    • Final round: Juriko dared Onato to escape a prison — a cell with a password lock
    • The only hint: the answer is a good story, perhaps one that hadn't occurred yet
    • Onato tried everything — memories, word combinations, invented words — nothing worked
    • Juriko offered surrender (admit he's not the most powerful) — Onato refused out of pride
    • Onato recognized the trap but his pride wouldn't let him quit

    Onato's Solution — The Creation of the Mierese¶

    1. Created a record of everything he'd tried
    2. Separated part of his essence and breathed life into it → first Mierese
    3. Made a pact: live freely, but upon death, tell Onato at least one new story
    4. If the story is new → paradise
    5. If not → stay with Onato helping crack the puzzle

    The first Mierese recited everything Onato told them → this became the Grand Tale

    The Grand Tale Today¶

    • Living document updated by generations of Lore Keepers
    • Incorporates the most relevant characters and events of the galaxy
    • Being featured in the Grand Tale is the supreme honor
    • Functions like a blockchain — distributed across Lore Keepers' memories

    The Living Planet Neuno¶

    A singularity — defies the laws of creation.

    Nature¶

    • Literally a living entity the size of a planet
    • 3000+ years old (carbon dating)
    • Origin Truth: In the galaxy's primordial era, a colossal space parasite collided with Neuno and "infected" the geological structure.
    • Mechanics: The planet seems alive because the parasite is alive, using the planet's crust/mantle as its organs.
    • Organs/vital functions originate from the southern pole (suggesting growth started there)

    Biology of Neuno¶

    • Photosynthesis: Gathers nutrition from its radiant blue sun
    • Immune system: Reacts to Mierese expansion — tsunamis, droughts, diseases, poisoned crops
    • Hibernation: Sleeps for months each year when planet is distant from the blue sun
    • Verdant jungles → desolates of dust and grass
    • Enormous animals shelter underground
    • Pace of life slows across entire ecosystem

    Environment¶

    • Hostile jungles, high temperatures, deadly species
    • Forced Mierese to organize in small, mobile groups
    • Core mentality: everything outside your group is dangerous and trying to get you

    Native Species & Resources¶

    • Arevok: Grazing animals, prized meat
    • Irvilyings: Flying creatures with rare essence (from nighttime flowers)
    • Azul grass: Blue plant from cold mountain peaks
    • Herbritids: Rare cave mushroom
    • Luminous flora and deadly predators throughout wild regions

    The Benevolent Explorers (~2377)¶

    • When Mierese were nearly annihilated, unknown alien explorers arrived
    • Terraformed part of Neuno, advanced Mierese science
    • Set the basis for first interstellar travel
    • Left and were never seen again
    • Taught the Mierese the value of helping others and cooperation
    • Who were they? — remains one of the great unsolved mysteries of Mierese history

    Chronological History¶

    • ~2377: The Benevolent Explorers arrive and uplift the Mierese (see above)
    • ~2392: Atheist Mierese found the Democratic Order of Kamec in Kamec Sector
    • ~2396: Mierese dream of a mineral-based flora — leads to discovery of Glowhaven (MRZ-31)
    • ~2399: Mierese begin exploration of dangerous Coral Nebula (MRZ-18)

    Culture & Values¶

    Core Principles (from the Grand Tale)¶

    1. Danger of pride — Onato is imprisoned by his pride; Mierese remind each other of this
    2. Value of freedom — Onato freed them to live however they wish
    3. Stories over possessions — "Only your memory and intelligence will help you before Onato"
    4. Humility — "Being humble will keep you free from your ego"
    5. Substance over form — Self-expression, secrets, adventures, skills, intelligence matter
    6. Irrelevance of protocol — Possessions, details, protocols, dogmas, precision are dismissed

    Language¶

    • No written language — believe written words fail to convey emotions and sensations
    • Some Mierese learn common written language for inter-faction interaction
    • Consider written communication "severely lacking, almost useless"
    • Heavy reliance on oral tradition and prestigious memory

    Clothing¶

    • Light, mobile clothing providing some protection
    • Different leathers from hunted animals; practical fibers
    • Dark tones preferred in unsafe environments (stealth)
    • Will use other species' clothing if practical
    • In safe environments, individual personality dictates style

    Sports & Entertainment¶

    • Motion control, personal skill, hand-to-hand combat, weapon combat
    • Favorite game: Narrative Battles — bard face-off (similar to rap battles)

    Society & Government¶

    Structure¶

    • Small groups and villages spread across Neuno's six regions
    • Local leadership handles common affairs — never intrudes on individual sphere
    • No central government until the Convergence War (forced by ONI Consortium pressure)
    • Government = loose coalition of notables with an elected Speaker (quasi prime minister)
    • Speaker's functions are limited — avoids general rules/laws unless necessary
    • Government viewed with suspicion — if Onato freed them, why should anyone dictate behavior?
    • Only viable because of Lore Keeper support
    • Currently leading the ONI Consortium — Central Space Station shows Mierese architecture/style

    The Hikibashi (Elite Special Forces)¶

    • Elite special forces unit of the Mierese
    • Trained assassins and spies, skilled in multiple combat arts
    • Think of them as extremely capable espionage operatives
    • A separate military institution from the civilian government

    Social Status (Story-Based)¶

    • Success = number of stories you appear in
    • Everyone belongs to: family story, possibly city story, craft story
    • Regional Lore Keeper selects who is worthy of bigger stories
    • Formal introduction requires reciting the stories you're part of

    The Non-Existent (Worst Punishment)¶

    • Being erased from all stories = worst fate in Mierese society
    • Non-existent cannot formally introduce themselves
    • Viewed as "worse than dead" by Mierese standards
    • Historically had no rights (could be killed); COP intervened to protect their lives
    • Religiously: non-existent never meet Onato, simply vanish after death

    The Lore Keepers¶

    The foundation of Mierese society.

    Role¶

    • Special class trained from childhood by elder Keepers
    • Guardians of the Grand Tale and collective memory
    • Religious support for communities
    • Select which stories enter the Grand Tale
    • Act as representatives of Onato in the physical realm
    • Only institution giving the government legitimacy

    Selection & Training¶

    • Potential candidates chosen by elder Keepers at young age
    • Learn the Grand Tale + memorization techniques for vast quantities of information
    • Young Keepers start with small community religious instruction
    • Earn voting rights on Grand Tale additions through merit

    Annual Grand Tale Assembly¶

    • Elder Lore Keepers reunite in an unknown location each Neuno year
    • Share collected stories
    • Vote on which should be incorporated into the Grand Tale
    • Distributed memory = if any Keeper dies, information survives with others

    Religion: Stories as Living Entities¶

    Core Beliefs¶

    • Stories exist in another plane with the gods — they are living entities
    • Stories exert influence over reality — the more famous, the greater the impact
    • "Stories are vessels for the universe's moral themes"
    • Both planes (divine and physical) influence each other cyclically

    Afterlife¶

    • Upon death: recite a new story to Onato
    • New story → paradise
    • No new story → stay with Onato, helping crack the puzzle
    • Non-existent → never meet Onato, simply vanish

    Spiritual Communication¶

    • Lore Keepers can communicate with spirits and their stories (medium sessions)
    • Souls of good/bad intentions inspire participants through indirect means
    • Excludes non-existent beings

    The Possession Risk (Onato Trap)¶

    • Extreme identification with a story can make a Mierese "possessed"
    • Becomes an avatar of their idea/story, willing to die for it
    • Can develop alternate personalities — the character they envision themselves as
    • May enable acts beyond their normal capabilities
    • Also causes problems: depression, despair, flipped moral values
    • Rare but always has significant historical impact when it occurs
    • Named after Onato's trap — pride imprisoning the self
