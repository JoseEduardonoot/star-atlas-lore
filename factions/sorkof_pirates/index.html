    "Every Jorvik captain dreams of being free. Sorkof actually is. That's why we hate him." â€” Intercepted Jorvik communications

    The Sorkof Pirates broke from the Jorvik when the Pirate King codified the Jorvik Creed â€” a set of rules that, in Sorkof's words, turned anarchists into bureaucrats. While the Jorvik are united under philosophical anarchism with codified rules (no slavery, no targeting civilians, honor among pirates), the Sorkof operate without ideology, without code, and without restraint.

    The key distinction: the Jorvik are ideological anarchists who oppose centralized governance on principle. The Sorkof are opportunistic criminals â€” pirates because it pays, not because it means something. Their willingness to cross every line makes them disproportionately feared. Where the Jorvik refuse to take slaves, the Sorkof built a business around it. Where the Jorvik spare civilians, the Sorkof see cargo. Where the Jorvik have a code, the Sorkof have a captain.

    History¶

    The Split (~2580)¶

    Grael Sorkof was a mid-ranking Jorvik captain â€” talented at raiding, terrible at ideology. When the Pirate King codified the Creed, Sorkof publicly rejected it: "Rules make you a government. I didn't become a pirate to join another government." The flashpoint was slavery â€” Sorkof had been running a quiet side operation selling captured crew to labor brokers. When the Creed banned it, he refused to comply.

    Sorkof took his crew and three additional ships and sailed out of Jorvik space. The Pirate King declared them "Creedless" â€” a kill-on-sight bounty for any Jorvik captain.

    The Capture of The Debt (~2585)¶

    Five years later, Sorkof's fleet intercepted a Crumon Dynasty heavy freighter making an unescorted run through outer Pavo Passage. After a fourteen-hour pursuit, Sorkof claimed it as his flagship, renaming it The Debt â€” "Everyone owes us something."

    Growth Through Infamy (~2585—Present)¶

    The fleet grew by recruiting Jorvik rejects, MRZ criminals, and desperate drifters. Their willingness to do anything for profit attracted the worst elements of MRZ spacefaring life. Strength fluctuates wildly with raid seasons. Despite the lack of ideology, a peculiar loyalty to Sorkof holds the fleet together â€” he has never failed to pay his crews.

    Tactics¶

    The Sorkof operate on a predator-prey model: scouts identify targets, fast ships disable engines and communications, The Debt boards and strips. If a target fights back effectively, they withdraw. They never attack military escorts head-on â€” never staying in one location longer than seventy-two hours.

    Survival Law¶

    "We don't have a creed. We have a captain." â€” Torvo Blackwake

    No written code â€” a deliberate rejection of everything the Jorvik stand for. Forty years of operations have produced informal realities:

    The Captain's Table: After every raid, Sorkof takes twenty percent. The rest is divided by vessel. Disputes are settled by Sorkof personally. His decisions are final.

    No Loyalty Without Payment: Crews serve because the pay is consistent. In over forty years, no Sorkof pirate has gone unpaid after a successful operation. This is the glue.

    The Only Rule: Never steal from the fleet. Internal theft is punished by spacing. Not morality â€” pragmatism.

    The Slave Pipeline¶

    The Sorkof run the galaxy's most brazen open pirate slave operation. During raids, crew with valuable skills are taken; the rest released or ransomed. Captives transit via Gullet to pre-arranged handoff points, sold to unregulated mining operations, Pavo Passage labor brokers, and private collectors. Slave sales account for an estimated thirty to forty percent of fleet income.

    This practice makes the Sorkof enemies of the Jorvik (ultimate Creed violation), the Garadar DAC (anti-slavery mission), and the COP.
