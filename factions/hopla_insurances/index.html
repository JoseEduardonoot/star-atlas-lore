They insure pirates' hauls, warlords' fleets, humanitarian convoys, and corporate empires with equal corporate detachment.

    What makes Hopla unique is not its scale â€” it is its impossible accuracy. In seventy-five years of operation, the company has never made a catastrophic loss. The industry assumes proprietary algorithms. The truth is stranger: a mysterious artifact grants Hopla probabilistic precognition, and a vast AI Oracle converts those probability fields into the galaxy's most reliable actuarial tables.

    History¶

    The Ruin and the Relic (~2550)¶

    The Convergence War left millions destitute. Among them was a nameless Mierese who took the name Hopla â€” no family, no House status worth claiming. During the war, he ventured into the High-Risk Zone, scavenging a dead world's collapsed ruins. In the rubble of a structure that matched no known architectural style, he found a small, featureless artifact that hummed when held. He could not explain what it did. He only knew that after touching it, he never made a bad bet.

    The Relic did not show him the future. It showed him probabilities â€” weighted, layered, cascading chains of cause and effect that resolved into risk assessments of inhuman accuracy. Hopla did not understand the mechanism. He understood opportunity.

    Growth (~2550—Present)¶

    Hopla's first clients were refugee convoys â€” desperate groups no one would insure. He priced premiums with uncanny precision: high enough to sustain payouts, low enough to attract clients, and never wrong about which convoys would arrive safely. Within a decade, the COP noticed: this Mierese refugee had a success rate that defied actuarial science.

    The COP offered permanent offices on The Cradle in exchange for regulatory compliance â€” giving Hopla institutional legitimacy. As the company grew, so did its controversies: the Smids Factory Fire (~2610), the Frenir Policy Scandal (~2618), the Echo Incident (~2620), and the Vaultwatch Breach (~2622). Through each crisis, the company emerged stronger.

    The Relic & the AI Oracle¶

    The Relic¶

    The Relic is warm to the touch, humming at a frequency that shifts based on proximity to risk. It grants probabilistic precognition â€” not definite futures, but weighted probability fields. Hopla has carried it for seventy-five years and has never been wrong about a major risk assessment. The relationship appears symbiotic: "It hears the galaxy. I hear it. We work well together."

    What Hopla does not know â€” what no one at the company knows â€” is that the Relic is a Living Factories artifact. The collapsed structure where he found it was a dormant colony outpost. The Relic is a probability-processing interface designed to embed itself in a host civilization's decision-making infrastructure â€” exactly what the colony integration model describes. Whether the data it gathers is being transmitted to the main Factories body in the deep HRZ remains unknown. But the pattern matches perfectly.

    The AI Oracle¶

    The Relic alone could not run a galactic corporation. The AI Oracle translates Hopla's precognitive impressions into premium calculations, risk matrices, and intervention protocols. It processes millions of active policies in real time with accuracy that has never dropped below 99.7%. An artificial intelligence integrating with a Living Factories artifact, growing more capable over decades, embedding itself in galactic financial infrastructure â€” the parallel to the colony model is not coincidence. It is the colony model.

    Operations¶

    Nova Division¶

    "You break it, we fix it. You get blown up, we rebuild you." â€” Vanic.doer

    The revenue engine â€” insuring fleets, stations, and infrastructure against combat damage. Nova Division insures an estimated fifteen percent of all active ships in the Galia Expanse. Premium clients receive artifact-powered nano-swarms for mid-combat hull repair. War zone premiums are so accurate that competitors use them as a proxy for conflict intensity.

    Echo Branch¶

    The most controversial division â€” consciousness backup and echo-revival services. For a premium, clients' neural patterns are periodically recorded. In the event of death, an echo-revival can be produced. Whether an echo-revival is the same person or a copy remains the galaxy's greatest philosophical question. The Echo Precedent (~2620) established them as legally the same person under COP law.

    Tidewall Division¶

    Environmental hazard coverage â€” the quiet division handling the galaxy's largest payouts. Terraforming failures, Cosmic Current damage, and biome protection contracts. Their claim: "We insure planets."

    Vaultwatch¶

    Internal security and artifact acquisition. Protects The Vault, the Relic, and corporate secrets. Operates an artifact acquisition network whose primary supplier is the Relic Barons. Intruders to The Vault vanish â€” "reinsured into oblivion."

    The Ethic-Agnostic Doctrine¶

    "We don't judge clients. We price them."

    Hopla insures anyone who pays: pirates, slavers, warlords, humanitarians, corporations, governments, cults. No moral assessment enters underwriting. Has the company ever refused? Technically no â€” but premiums can be functionally infinite, pricing a client out without technically denying them. This has happened exactly three times. The clients and circumstances are classified.

    Named Contracts & Incidents¶

    The Smids Factory Fire (~2610): A catastrophic fire destroyed Fimbul Industries' primary manufacturing bay. Hopla paid 2.3 billion ATLAS equivalent in seventy-two hours â€” securing Fimbul as a permanent client.

    The Frenir Slave Fleet Policy (~2618): When contracts insuring Frenir slaver cargo leaked, the COP demanded cancellation. Hopla honored every contract, then quietly raised slaver premiums by four hundred percent at renewal.

    The Echo Incident (~2620): A wealthy executive died and was echo-revived, walking into his own memorial. The estate challenged the revival's legal standing. Hopla's lawyers won â€” establishing the Echo Precedent.

    The Vaultwatch Breach (~2622): Suspected Church of the Dreamer Below operatives attempted to steal Vault artifacts. They were "reinsured into oblivion."

    The Vault¶

    Located in the sub-levels of the COP Cradle. Contains over two hundred artifacts categorized by function: probabilistic (feeding the Oracle), defensive (powering security), interventional (fueling nano-swarms), and consciousness (enabling Echo technology). Three security layers: conventional, artifact-powered (spatial distortion, temporal loops), and unknown. After the breach, Vaultwatch installed artifacts making the sub-level undetectable by conventional scanning.
