    🤖 Canon Document: KAMEC (MRZ-29)¶

    Status: 🟢 Canon (Creator-provided, Feb 2026) Source: Direct Creator input — Kamec lore document + Archon Deep-Dive + Origin lore nuggets Zone: Medium Risk Zone (MRZ-29) Influence: ONI Theme: Clone Democracy, Alien Technology, Hidden Divergence, Elegant Dystopia

    Overview¶

    The Kamec Sector, presided over by the Democratic Order of Kamec, is a bastion of two unwavering principles: Democracy and Order. "Kamec" means "equal" — a name that may no longer reflect reality.

    History¶

    Phase 1: The Founding (~2392)¶

    • ~2392: A group of atheist Mierese who decided to live free from their species' religious rules found their way to a bountiful planet on MRZ-29
    • They established the Democratic Order of Kamec — where all members had a say and a vote
    • "Kamec" = "equal" in their language
    • Appealed to individuals from all species and factions looking to flee recruitment and the looming war
    • Kamec quickly grew into a regional safe haven

    Phase 2: The Stagnation¶

    • Kamec became stagnated under the bureaucratic demands of pure democracy
    • Citizens spent a large part of their days just voting on different propositions
    • The system was ideologically pure but practically unsustainable

    Phase 3: The Alien Technology (~2513)¶

    • ~2513: An explorer from the HRZ sold Kamec's government a strange piece of technology
    • Originated from a collapsed empire in the depths of Galia
    • Claimed it would allow a breakthrough for their governance

    Phase 4: The Clones (~2519)¶

    • ~2519: Using the HRZ technology, Kamec developed perfect imitations of their citizens
    • These clones share the same memories as their originals
    • Deployed to vote on decisions on behalf of their original counterparts
    • Initially the system worked — clones accurately reflected citizens' will

    Phase 5: The Divergence (Current — SECRET)¶

    [!CAUTION] The following is canon truth but NOT fully understood in-universe. Strange rumors circulate, but the reality is hidden.

    • The clone-agents have begun to diverge from their originals' will
    • They no longer reliably represent the citizens who spawned them
    • Citizens mostly don't realize this is happening — votes are secret
    • Reports of clones not abiding by their originals' wills have spread through news channels
    • ~2621: The Kamec Democratic Order publicly denies the rumors — strange reports continue to surface
    • The suspiciously precise 85.7623% opposing external scrutiny is a narrative red flag — likely fabricated by divergent clones protecting themselves

    The 85.7623% (Narrative Red Flag)¶

    • Kamec has rebuffed ALL external inspection of their system — including the COP
    • Their defense: exactly 85.7623% of their populace opposes scrutiny
    • This precision is deliberately suspicious — authentic democratic results don't produce such exact numbers
    • It's a pista narrativa (narrative clue): the clones are generating falsified results
    • The rest of Galia remains in perpetual wonder about the authenticity

    Economy: Crystals & Gems¶

    • The sector is a treasure trove of valuable crystals and gems
    • Mining permitted for outsiders with:
    • A deal with the Kamec government
    • Adherence to mandated fees
    • Visitors and traders welcomed — no threat to democracy and order tolerated
    • (Specific materials TBD — Creator will populate later)

    If Exposed: Galactic Crisis¶

    [!WARNING] If someone audits the code and discovers the clone divergence, it would trigger a major galactic crisis — likely causing intervention by the ONI Consortium or even the Council of Peace.

    • The precedent: a supposedly perfect democracy revealed as a fraud
    • Would undermine trust in AI/clone governance across Galia
    • Could become a catalyst for conflict — potentially explosive as the Free Harbors tension

    Narrative Hooks¶

    • Black Mirror in Space: A society that outsourced democracy to clones who are now voting for themselves
    • The HRZ Tech: What collapsed empire created clone technology? What else did they create?
    • The Whistleblower: Someone discovering the divergence — the galaxy's biggest exposé since the Vega Fall documentary
    • Clone Rights: If the clones are sentient and diverging, are they people? Do they have rights?
    • Secret Election: Citizens trusting secret ballots they didn't actually cast
